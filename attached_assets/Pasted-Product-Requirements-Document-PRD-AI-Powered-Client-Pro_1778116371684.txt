Product Requirements Document (PRD)
AI-Powered Client Proposal & Invoice Generator
Document Version: 1.0 Last Updated: May 6, 2026 Product Name: ProposalForge (working title) Document Owner: Product / Engineering Status: Draft — Pending Approval

1. Executive Summary
ProposalForge is a SaaS web application that helps freelancers and small agencies turn a plain-text description of a project into a polished client-ready proposal, itemized quote, and invoice in under sixty seconds. The core differentiator is AI-driven content generation powered by the Gemini API, wrapped in a workflow that handles editing, branding, PDF export, email delivery, and payment collection.

The product targets a well-documented pain point: freelancers routinely spend two to four hours per proposal, often losing leads to slow turnaround. ProposalForge compresses that cycle into minutes while producing output that feels custom, not templated.

Beyond its commercial intent, this project also serves as a portfolio centerpiece, demonstrating end-to-end full-stack capability across AI integration, relational data modeling, document generation, transactional email, authentication, and payment processing.

2. Goals and Success Metrics
The primary product goal is to enable any freelancer to generate, customize, and send a professional proposal in under five minutes from sign-up. Secondary goals include establishing a recurring revenue stream via subscription tiers and producing a working public demo that converts portfolio visitors into client leads.

Success will be measured against the following targets within the first ninety days of public launch: at least five hundred sign-ups, a free-to-paid conversion rate of five percent or higher, a median time-to-first-proposal under four minutes, an AI output acceptance rate (proposals sent without major edits) above sixty percent, and a portfolio-to-inquiry conversion lift of at least twenty percent attributable to the live demo.

3. Target Users and Personas
The primary persona is the solo freelancer — designers, developers, copywriters, consultants, and marketers earning between thirty thousand and one hundred fifty thousand dollars annually, who currently cobble proposals together in Google Docs or Notion. They want speed, professionalism, and a way to look like a bigger operation than they are.

The secondary persona is the boutique agency owner managing two to ten contractors, who needs consistent branding across proposals authored by different team members and wants visibility into which proposals have been viewed and accepted.

A tertiary persona is the portfolio visitor / prospective client who lands on the developer's portfolio, tries the live demo, and either signs up or reaches out for custom development work.

4. Problem Statement
Freelancers lose deals because proposals take too long to write and frequently look amateur compared to those produced by larger agencies. Existing solutions fall into two camps: heavyweight CRMs like HoneyBook or Bonsai that bundle proposals into broader practice-management suites and require significant onboarding, or static template marketplaces that produce generic output. Neither offers true AI-assisted drafting tailored to a freelancer's described scope, and neither connects proposal acceptance directly to invoice generation and payment in a single fluid workflow.

5. Product Scope
5.1 In Scope for V1 (MVP)
The MVP will include user authentication with email/password and Google OAuth, a project intake form where users describe scope in natural language along with structured fields for budget range, timeline, and client details, AI-powered generation of three artifacts (proposal narrative, itemized quote, and draft invoice), an inline rich-text editor for refining AI output, branding customization (logo upload, accent color, business details), PDF export with professional formatting, email delivery to clients via transactional email service, a client-facing acceptance page where recipients can view and accept proposals with a digital signature, automatic invoice generation upon acceptance, Stripe-powered payment collection on invoices, a dashboard showing all proposals and their statuses, and subscription billing with a free tier (three proposals per month) and a Pro tier (unlimited).

5.2 Out of Scope for V1
Deferred to later releases: team/multi-seat accounts, native mobile apps, e-signature compliance certifications (DocuSign-level), CRM integrations (HubSpot, Pipedrive), recurring invoices and subscriptions for end clients, multi-currency tax automation beyond basic VAT/sales tax fields, white-label reseller accounts, and an AI chatbot for client questions on proposals.

6. User Stories and Functional Requirements
6.1 Authentication and Onboarding
A new user can sign up with email and password or Google OAuth and is guided through a three-step onboarding that collects business name, logo, and default currency. Returning users land directly on the dashboard. Password reset is supported via emailed magic link. Sessions are JWT-based with refresh token rotation.

6.2 Proposal Creation Flow
The user clicks "New Proposal" and is presented with an intake form containing a large free-text field labeled "Describe the project" plus structured inputs for client name, client email, estimated budget, timeline, and project type (dropdown: web development, design, marketing, consulting, other). On submission, the backend constructs a structured prompt and calls the Gemini API, returning within ten seconds a generated proposal containing an executive summary, scope of work, deliverables, timeline with milestones, an itemized quote with line items and prices, terms and conditions, and a call to action. The user is then dropped into an editor view with the generated content pre-populated and fully editable.

6.3 Editing and Customization
The editor presents the proposal in sectioned blocks (summary, scope, deliverables, timeline, quote table, terms) that can each be edited in place. Line items in the quote table can be added, removed, or reordered, and totals recalculate automatically. A side panel shows branding controls (logo, color, font choice from a curated set of three) and a live preview that reflects what the client will see. Users can regenerate any individual section with AI by clicking a "Regenerate" button on that block, optionally providing a steering instruction such as "make it more formal" or "shorten by half."

6.4 Sending and Acceptance
The user clicks "Send to Client," reviews recipient details, optionally adds a personal message, and confirms. The system emails the client a link to a hosted, branded acceptance page where they can read the proposal, download a PDF copy, and either accept (with typed-name signature) or request changes via a comment thread. The freelancer receives email notifications on view, accept, and comment events. Upon acceptance, the system automatically generates an invoice based on the agreed quote and emails it to the client with a Stripe payment link.

6.5 Invoice and Payment
Invoices are first-class entities, viewable and editable from the dashboard. Each invoice has a unique number (configurable prefix), itemized lines, tax fields, due date, and payment status. Stripe Checkout handles card payments; on successful payment, the invoice is marked paid and both parties are notified. Stripe webhooks reconcile payment state. The freelancer can also manually mark an invoice as paid (for off-platform payments like bank transfer).

6.6 Dashboard and Management
The dashboard lists all proposals and invoices with filters by status (draft, sent, viewed, accepted, declined, paid, overdue), search by client name, and quick actions (duplicate, archive, download PDF, resend). Summary cards at the top show monthly revenue, outstanding invoice total, and proposal acceptance rate.

6.7 Subscription and Billing
The free tier allows three proposals per calendar month with ProposalForge branding on the client-facing page. The Pro tier ($19/month or $190/year) removes branding, allows unlimited proposals, unlocks custom fonts and color presets, and enables the comment thread on acceptance pages. Stripe Billing handles subscription lifecycle. Users hitting the free-tier limit see an in-app upgrade prompt.

7. Non-Functional Requirements
The system must serve dashboard pages in under one second on a median connection and complete AI generation in under fifteen seconds at the ninety-fifth percentile. Uptime target is ninety-nine point five percent. All data in transit uses TLS 1.2 or higher; passwords are hashed with bcrypt or argon2; Stripe and Gemini API keys are stored in a secrets manager, never in source. The application must be responsive across desktop, tablet, and mobile viewports, with full functionality on screens four hundred pixels wide and above. Accessibility should target WCAG 2.1 AA conformance. The system should be GDPR-friendly: users can export and delete their data on request.

8. Technical Architecture
The frontend will be built in React with TypeScript, using Vite for the build toolchain, TailwindCSS for styling, shadcn/ui for primitive components, TanStack Query for server state, and React Hook Form with Zod for form validation. The rich-text editor will use Tiptap. PDF preview rendering will use react-pdf or a server-rendered fallback.

The backend will be Node.js with TypeScript on the Express or Fastify framework, exposing a REST API (with a versioned prefix, e.g. /api/v1). PostgreSQL will be the primary datastore, accessed via Prisma ORM. Redis will handle session storage, rate limiting, and a job queue (BullMQ) for background tasks like PDF generation and email sending.

PDFs will be generated server-side using Puppeteer rendering an HTML template, which gives pixel-perfect output and matches the on-screen preview. Transactional email will go through Resend or Postmark. AI generation will call the Gemini API with a structured prompt template and JSON schema response mode to ensure parseable output. Payments use Stripe Checkout for one-time invoice payments and Stripe Billing for subscriptions, with webhook endpoints handling state changes.

The deployment target is Vercel for the frontend and Railway or Fly.io for the backend and Postgres, with Cloudflare R2 or AWS S3 for logo and PDF asset storage. CI/CD runs on GitHub Actions with automated tests on pull requests and preview deployments per branch.

9. Data Model (High-Level)
The core entities are User (id, email, hashed password, business profile, plan), Client (id, owner user, name, email, company), Proposal (id, owner, client, status, content blocks as JSONB, created/sent/accepted timestamps), QuoteLineItem (id, proposal, description, quantity, unit price), Invoice (id, proposal, number, line items, status, due date, Stripe payment intent), AcceptanceEvent (id, proposal, type, timestamp, signer name, IP), and Subscription (id, user, Stripe subscription id, plan, status, current period end). Soft deletes are used for proposals and invoices to preserve audit history.

10. AI Prompt Strategy
Generation calls send Gemini a system prompt establishing the role ("You are a senior proposal writer for freelancers") plus a structured user prompt containing the project description, client details, budget, timeline, and the freelancer's business profile. The model is instructed to return strict JSON conforming to a schema covering each proposal section, with prices as numbers and dates as ISO strings. Temperature is set around 0.7 for creativity in narrative sections and lowered to 0.3 for the quote breakdown. Output is validated with Zod before persisting; on validation failure, one automatic retry is attempted before surfacing an error.

A library of prompt variants will be maintained per project type (web dev, design, marketing, consulting) to produce more idiomatic output. All prompts are versioned in code so generation quality can be A/B tested over time.

11. Milestones and Timeline
The build is planned across roughly eight to ten weeks of focused solo work. Weeks one and two cover project scaffolding, authentication, and the user/client data model. Weeks three and four focus on AI integration, the proposal intake form, and the editor. Weeks five and six implement PDF generation, email sending, and the client acceptance page. Week seven adds invoicing and Stripe integration. Week eight covers the subscription tier, billing UI, and polish. Weeks nine and ten handle bug fixes, accessibility audit, deployment hardening, landing page, and public launch.

12. Risks and Mitigations
The largest technical risk is AI output quality and consistency; mitigation is structured JSON schema responses, per-vertical prompt variants, and a regenerate-with-instructions feature so users always have an escape hatch. Cost risk from AI API usage is mitigated with per-user generation rate limits and tiered quotas. PDF rendering with Puppeteer can be operationally heavy; this is mitigated by isolating it in a worker queue and caching rendered outputs. Stripe integration carries compliance risk, addressed by using Stripe Checkout (which handles PCI scope) rather than custom card forms. Finally, deliverability for client-facing emails is mitigated by using a reputable transactional provider with proper SPF, DKIM, and DMARC configuration on the sending domain.

13. Open Questions
Several decisions remain to be confirmed before development begins: whether to support multiple currencies at launch or defer to V2, whether the free tier should watermark the PDF or only the hosted acceptance page, what the exact pricing should be for the Pro tier after competitive analysis, and whether to offer a lifetime deal on launch via AppSumo or similar to seed early users and reviews.

14. Appendix — Portfolio Positioning
As a portfolio piece, the live demo URL should be linked prominently from the developer's site with a one-line value prop ("Generate client proposals in 60 seconds with AI") and a frictionless try-without-signup mode that lets visitors generate one sample proposal anonymously before being prompted to create an account. This maximizes the chance that prospective clients viewing the portfolio experience the product directly, which is consistently more persuasive than screenshots or case-study copy.
